Framing your project in a story format can help refine your ideas by giving it a human touch. What I mean is that the storytelling approach to web design can help your site feel much more human. Creative Brief

  • Problem: There's not an easy way to find retro game stores near you.
  • Audience: Those who like retro game collecting in actual stores rather than just buying them online.
  • Competition: Sites like PriceCharting, VideoGameStores, and 2ndHandGeek all do something similar to my idea, in various fields with varying degrees of success.
  • Key Messages: With this project, it will help those out there who love the thrill of going to the store to buy video games, and cater to those who love collecting older titles to play or collect, while also giving highlight to small independent businesses in their area who specialize in this field.
  • Call to Action: Come use the site to see what retro game shops are in your area, and give them the business they deserve for working in a bit of a niche market.
  • How to Measure Success: Have feedback from users, whether it's a place where they can discuss any cool retro game stores they've discovered in their area thanks to the site, or shout outs to any small businesses who have been given more public knowledge due to the site.
